The orthodox stance is the starting point for all boxing technique. A sound stance gives you balance for both offence and defence, protects your centreline, and puts you in position to throw and receive punches efficiently.

## Steps

1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, then step your left foot forward so your feet are staggered roughly one shoulder-width in depth.
2. Angle your body about 45° to your opponent so your left side faces them — this narrows your target profile.
3. Bend both knees slightly and distribute your weight evenly across both feet; stay on the balls of your feet.
4. Raise both hands: left fist in front of your left cheek (lead hand), right fist beside your right cheek (rear hand). Elbows tucked down to protect the ribs.
5. Keep your chin slightly tucked and look over the knuckles of your lead hand.

## Key Points

- The rear heel lifts slightly off the ground, keeping you light on your feet.
- Shoulders are relaxed — tension kills speed.
- The lead shoulder angles forward, providing passive defence for the chin.
- Keep elbows close to the body; flared elbows expose the liver and ribs.
- This stance applies to right-handed (orthodox) fighters. Left-handed fighters mirror it (southpaw).

## Common Mistakes

- Standing square to the opponent, presenting the full chest as a target.
- Feet too narrow or too wide, destroying balance under pressure.
- Hands held too low or too wide, leaving the face unprotected.
- Locking the knees, which prevents explosive movement and tires the legs.
- Chin raised, inviting a knockout punch to land cleanly.
