The cross is thrown with the rear hand (right hand in orthodox) in a straight line, generating power from the ground up through the legs, hips, and core. Unlike the jab, the cross is a primary power punch and the backbone of most combinations.

## Steps

1. From your orthodox stance, initiate the cross by pushing off the rear foot and pivoting on the ball of that foot.
2. Rotate your hips and torso toward the target — the power comes from this rotation, not the arm alone.
3. Extend the rear hand straight toward the target, rotating the fist palm-down at full extension while the shoulder drives forward.
4. Keep the lead hand tight to your cheek to protect from a counter jab.
5. Snap the rear hand back as the hip rotation reverses, returning immediately to your guard.

## Key Points

- The pivot of the rear foot is where the power originates — no pivot, no power.
- The shoulder should brush past your chin as the punch extends, protecting it from counters.
- Drive through the target, not just to it, for maximum penetration.
- The cross works best after a jab has occupied your opponent's attention (jab-cross is the fundamental combination).
- Body crosses to the liver are just as effective as head crosses — vary your levels.

## Common Mistakes

- Arm-punching without hip rotation — the punch looks right but has no power.
- Dropping the lead hand during the throw, leaving the face open to a counter jab.
- Over-rotating and losing balance after the punch lands.
- Telegraphing by winding up the shoulder before throwing.
- Throwing a cross from too far away, arriving without power at the target.
