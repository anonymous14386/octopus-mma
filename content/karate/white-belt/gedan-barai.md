Gedan Barai (downward sweeping block) is the foundational low block of karate. It deflects kicks and low strikes directed at the lower body by driving the forearm in a powerful downward arc across the centerline and finishing below the belt. It is the opening technique of Heian Shodan (the first kata) and appears in virtually every kata in the Shotokan, Goju-ryu, and Shito-ryu systems. The block is always paired with a sharp reaction hand pull and is executed in front stance (Zenkutsu-dachi) in kata.

## Steps

1. Begin in Zenkutsu-dachi (front stance) or Yoi (ready position).
2. Wind up: raise the blocking arm to the opposite shoulder, crossing in front of the body. The fist is near the opposite ear, elbow pointing outward, palm facing inward toward the head.
3. Simultaneously raise the other arm as the reaction arm — it will pull back to the hip.
4. Drive the blocking arm downward in a sweeping arc: the forearm sweeps across the body and finishes low — fist approximately 10cm above the front thigh, on the outside of the lead leg.
5. Pull the reaction hand sharply back to the hip, palm-up (Hikite).
6. Kime (focus): tighten the arm, fist, and core sharply at the endpoint.

## Key Points

- The finishing position is low — the fist is near the thigh, not at the hip or navel. The forearm angles downward across the front thigh.
- The blocking surface is the outer forearm (the same side as the little finger).
- The wind-up (raising the arm to the opposite shoulder) creates the arc that generates sweeping power.
- Hikite (the reaction hand pulling back to the hip) is as important as the block itself — it creates the counter-rotation that doubles the speed.
- In Shotokan kata, Gedan Barai is executed with kiai and maximum kime at the endpoint.

## Common Mistakes

- Finishing with the fist too high (at hip or navel level) rather than near the thigh.
- Blocking straight downward rather than diagonally across the body — the block must cross the centerline to deflect an attack from in front.
- Weak or absent Hikite — the reaction hand pull is not optional.
- Tensing the arm from the beginning of the swing — the arm should be relaxed through the arc, sharp only at kime.
- Dropping the elbow so the forearm ends up nearly horizontal rather than angled downward.
