Oi Zuki (lunge punch) is the fundamental stepping punch of karate. Like the Taekwondo straight punch, the striking hand is on the same side as the lead foot — the "oi" (chasing or following) hand. What distinguishes it from simply punching while stepping is the Shotokan emphasis on synchronising the full body momentum of the step with the delivery of the punch: the fist arrives at the target at the precise moment the front foot lands. This timing converts the mass and momentum of the entire stepping body into the strike.

## Steps

1. Begin in Zenkutsu-dachi (left leg forward), right fist extended from a previous technique, left fist chambered at the left hip.
2. Step forward with the right foot, moving into right Zenkutsu-dachi.
3. As the right foot travels forward, chamber the right fist back to the right hip, palm-up.
4. As the right foot lands, drive the right fist forward in a straight line to the target (Chudan — solar plexus level, or Jodan — face level).
5. The fist rotates from palm-up to palm-down during the punch — the rotation completes at the moment of impact.
6. Pull the left fist sharply back to the left hip simultaneously (Hikite).
7. Kime as the foot lands and the fist reaches full extension — the whole body locks for an instant.

## Key Points

- Timing is the essence: the punch and the footfall must be simultaneous — not the punch before, not after.
- The travel of the punching fist is a straight line — it does not arc or swing wide.
- The elbow tracks close to the ribs during the punch and ends pointing directly backward.
- The fist rotation happens at the very end of the extension, producing a snap rather than a rotation-throughout punch.
- The body drops slightly as the front foot lands into the deep Zenkutsu-dachi — this drop adds mass to the impact.

## Common Mistakes

- Punching before the step completes — the arm arrives early and the body momentum is wasted.
- Letting the elbow flare outward during the punch rather than tracking past the ribs.
- Starting the wrist rotation too early, losing the final snap.
- Insufficient Hikite — the reaction hand pull generates the counter-rotation that doubles the speed of the striking arm.
- Stepping without dropping into the stance — punching from a standing position rather than the deep Zenkutsu-dachi removes all the body-drop contribution to power.
