The middle block (Momtong Makgi), also called the inner middle block (Momtong An Makgi), deflects punches and strikes directed at the torso. The forearm sweeps across from the outside inward, redirecting the attack to the side of the body rather than absorbing it frontally. It appears frequently in Taeguek poomsae, particularly Taeguek Il Jang and Ee Jang.

## Steps

1. Begin in walking stance or fighting stance.
2. Wind up by raising the blocking arm — the fist starts at ear level on the same side, palm facing inward toward the head, elbow pointing outward.
3. Drive the forearm inward and slightly downward in an arc across the centerline of the body.
4. Simultaneously pull the reaction hand sharply to the opposite hip, palm-up.
5. Finish with the forearm vertical (or slightly angled), the fist at shoulder height on the blocking side, elbow bent at roughly 90°. The palm faces inward (toward the body).
6. Kime at full extension.

## Key Points

- The blocking surface is the inside of the forearm (the thumb side / radial bone).
- The fist finishes at the same height as the shoulder — not at face level, not at navel level.
- The elbow should be about a fist-width from the body — do not press the elbow into the ribs.
- The forearm should be roughly vertical at the end, not horizontal — the angle deflects a punch out to the side.
- The winding motion is important: starting from ear height generates the arc that creates deflective power.

## Common Mistakes

- Finishing with the fist too high (at face level) — this leaves the body still exposed.
- Finishing with the fist in front of the face centre rather than aligned over the blocking side shoulder.
- Blocking straight forward like a push rather than sweeping across the centerline.
- Forgetting the reaction hand, which halves the speed and snap of the block.
- Tensing the arm from the start — the arm should be relaxed during the arc, tight only at kime.
