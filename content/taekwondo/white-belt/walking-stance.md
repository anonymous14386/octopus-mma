The walking stance (Ap Seogi) is the fundamental stance of Taekwondo poomsae practice. While not used in sparring, it is the platform from which all beginner forms (Taeguek Il Jang through Sam Jang) are performed. Correct walking stance trains hip alignment, weight distribution, and the coordination of upper and lower body in combination with blocks and strikes.

## Steps

1. Stand with feet together (Charyot, attention stance) as your starting position.
2. Step forward with one foot approximately one shoulder-width ahead, keeping the feet parallel (both pointing forward — unlike a wider lunge).
3. Distribute your weight evenly — 50% on each foot.
4. Bend the front knee very slightly — the stance is upright, not deeply bent like a front stance.
5. Keep the hips squared forward, not rotated to the side.
6. Hold the upper body upright with the head up, chin level.

## Key Points

- The feet should be roughly shoulder-width apart both laterally and forward — narrower than a front stance (Apkubi).
- Both feet point directly forward, parallel to each other.
- The stance is mobile — you should be able to step forward naturally from it, as the name implies.
- Weight stays central; this is a transitional and form stance, not a rooted power stance.
- In Taeguek poomsae, each step into walking stance is coordinated with a block — the block should land at the same moment as the foot.

## Common Mistakes

- Making the stance too long (confusing it with front stance / Apkubi), which causes the knee to bend too deeply.
- Turning the feet outward rather than keeping them parallel and forward.
- Shifting weight unevenly forward onto the front foot.
- Losing the upright posture — the torso should not lean forward.
- Failing to coordinate the upper and lower body — the block should time with the step, not before or after.
