The straight punch (Baro Jireugi) is one of the first hand techniques taught in Taekwondo poomsae. Unlike the reverse punch where the rear hand strikes, the straight punch drives with the front hand — the same side as the lead foot. It is executed at the end of a forward step in walking stance, with the force of the step contributing to the impact.

## Steps

1. Begin in left walking stance (left foot forward) with fists at hip level — left fist palm-up at the left hip, right fist extended forward from a previous technique.
2. Step forward with the right foot into right walking stance while simultaneously chambering the right fist back to the right hip, palm-up.
3. As the right foot lands, drive the right fist forward in a straight line toward the target (solar plexus height), rotating the fist so the palm faces downward at full extension.
4. Simultaneously pull the left fist sharply back to the left hip, palm-up — this is the reaction hand (Bandae).
5. Lock (kime) at the moment of full extension — the forearm, wrist, and shoulder tighten to maximise transfer of force.

## Key Points

- The punch must travel in a perfectly straight line — not an arc or haymaker swing.
- The wrist rotates 180° during the punch: palm-up at the hip, palm-down at extension. The rotation happens at the very end of the movement.
- The elbow tracks close to the body and scrapes past the ribs rather than winging out to the side.
- Kime (focus) is essential — a relaxed arm through the motion, a sharp tighten at impact.
- In poomsae, the punch lands precisely as the stepping foot touches the ground.

## Common Mistakes

- Starting the wrist rotation too early, losing the snap at the end.
- Letting the elbow flare out during the punch, reducing power and alignment.
- Striking too high (face level) or too low (below the belt) rather than at solar plexus height.
- Not pulling back the reaction hand — without it, the punch slows down and loses half its power.
- Punching before the step completes, disconnecting arm from body momentum.
