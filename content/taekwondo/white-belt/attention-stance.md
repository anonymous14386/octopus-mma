The attention stance (Charyeot Seogi) is not a fighting or movement stance — it is a posture of readiness, respect, and discipline. It is held when the instructor calls "Charyeot!" (Attention!) at the beginning and end of class, before bowing (Gyeong-nye), and any time the practitioner is standing at rest but in a formal context. Correct attention stance communicates focus and respect.

## Steps

1. Stand upright with heels touching or nearly touching.
2. Turn the toes out equally so that the feet form a V-shape at approximately 45° total (22.5° each side from the centerline).
3. Lock the knees straight — legs are fully extended, not bent.
4. Let the arms hang naturally at the sides, hands loosely closed into light fists or open, fingers together.
5. Keep the chin level, eyes forward, shoulders back and relaxed, spine straight.

## Key Points

- Heels are together (or touching) — this is the key distinction from ready stance.
- The V-angle of the feet is modest — not a wide splay. Roughly 45° total.
- The body is upright but relaxed, not rigidly tensed.
- Arms hang at the sides — do not clasp hands in front or behind the body.
- This stance is held still; fidgeting or looking around is considered disrespectful.

## Common Mistakes

- Placing the feet parallel (heels together, toes forward) — this is closer to military attention but not correct for Taekwondo.
- Exaggerating the V so the feet are nearly inline — too wide a splay looks sloppy.
- Bending the knees slightly — the legs should be straight.
- Tensing the shoulders and neck — attention stance is alert, not rigid.
- Failing to hold still — the point of the stance is composure and focus.
