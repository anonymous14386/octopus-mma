The front kick (Ap Chagi) is the first kick taught in Taekwondo and forms the basis for all more advanced kicking techniques. Mastering the front kick develops hip flexibility, balance, and the muscle memory of chambering the knee before extending.

## Steps

1. Begin in your fighting stance with your non-kicking foot forward and hands raised in guard position.
2. Shift your weight onto your supporting leg and raise your kicking knee up toward your chest — this is the "chamber" position.
3. From the chamber, snap the foot forward and extend the leg, striking with the ball of the foot (the padded area just below the toes).
4. Retract the foot back to the chambered position immediately after contact — do not drop the leg straight down.
5. Return to fighting stance by placing the kicking foot either forward (stepping through) or back to its starting position.

## Key Points

- Keep the toes pulled back to expose the ball of the foot as the striking surface.
- The kicking knee should rise as high as possible before extension — the power comes from the snap, not a swing.
- Keep your guard up throughout the kick; do not drop your hands.
- Stay on the ball of your supporting foot to allow hip rotation.
- The kick should be a sharp snap, not a push — retract fully.

## Common Mistakes

- Dropping the hands during the kick, exposing the face to a counter.
- Not chambering the knee before kicking — this reduces power and telegraphs the technique.
- Striking with the toes instead of the ball of the foot, risking injury.
- Leaning backward excessively, which reduces balance and power.
- Kicking from the ground without raising the knee first (a "swooping" kick).
